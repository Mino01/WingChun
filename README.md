## Key Findings

### The Wooden Dummy Form

The Muk Yan Jong form consists of **116 movements** divided into **8 sections**, as taught by Grandmaster Yip Man. The form serves multiple purposes:

- Teaching proper positioning and footwork
- Developing efficient transitions between techniques
- Conditioning bones and muscles through repetitive contact
- Providing physical feedback for structural correction
- Integrating all Wing Chun principles into practical application

### Body Development Principles

Wing Chun transforms the body into a weapon through:

1. **Structural Alignment** - The Yee Gee Kim Yeung Ma stance creates a triangular base that allows power to flow from the ground through the body
2. **Bone Hardening** - Based on Wolff's Law, controlled stress creates micro-fractures that heal stronger
3. **Muscle Conditioning** - Progressive training builds resilient muscles and tendons
4. **Internal Power** - Chi Sao and forms training develop sensitivity and explosive force

### Scientific Evidence

Research published in the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) database found that middle-aged Wing Chun practitioners, compared to active non-practitioners, had:

- **11.5% higher bone mineral density** in the radius
- **17.8% higher leg lean mass**
- **56.4% greater peak torque** in knee extensors
- **60.8% greater peak torque** in knee flexors

## Training Progression

The Wing Chun system follows a logical progression:

1. **Sil Lim Tao** (Little Idea) - Develops structure and fundamental techniques
2. **Chum Kiu** (Seeking the Bridge) - Adds movement, footwork, and body mass
3. **Biu Jee** (Thrusting Fingers) - Teaches emergency techniques and recovery
4. **Muk Yan Jong** (Wooden Dummy) - Integrates all principles with physical feedback
5. **Luk Dim Boon Gwun** (Dragon Pole) - Builds tremendous strength and long-range power
6. **Bart Cham Dao** (Butterfly Knives) - Develops wrist strength and short-range power
