# Wing Chun Wooden Dummy Techniques - Research Notes

## Source: 116 Wing Tsun Dummy Techniques by Grandmaster Yip Man
**Author:** Master Yip Chun (son of Grandmaster Yip Man)
**Technical Adviser:** Master Leung Ting
**Publication:** Leung's Publications, Hong Kong (1981)

## Overview
This is the authoritative source on wooden dummy techniques as demonstrated by Grandmaster Yip Man himself. The book contains 116 distinct techniques organized into sections.

## Key Techniques Identified (from visual analysis):

### Section 1: Opening Techniques (Movements 1-10)
1. **Right Bong-Sau** - Withdrawing right arm to pose Bong-sau to press at the right dummy arm
2. **Left Tan-Sau & Right Lower Lying-Palm** - Inserting left leg behind dummy leg while posing left Tan-sau and right Lower Lying-palm to strike at left side of dummy trunk
3. **Right Tan-Sau & Left Lower Lying-Palm** - Inserting right leg behind dummy leg while posing right Tan-sau and left Lower Lying-palm to strike at right side of dummy trunk
4. **High & Low Gaun-Sau** - Withdrawing leg to resume original position while posing arms in High & Low Gaun-sau gesture
5. **Right Kau-Sau & Left Tok-Sau** - Turning stance to face the dummy while posing right arm in Kau-sau and left arm in Tok-sau gesture
6. **Left Jut-Sau & Right Erect-Palm** - Converting right arm into Erect-palm and quickly thrusting it forward while changing left arm to Jut-sau to press heavily on dummy arm

### Section 2: Opposite Side Techniques (Movements 11-20)
- Movements enacted at the opposite side of the dummy
- Includes mirror techniques of Section 1

### Additional Techniques Noted:
- **Indoor-Area Pak-Sau** (Movements 21-23) - Slapping palm at dummy arm from indoor area
- **Outdoor-Area Pak-Sau** (Movement 24) - Slapping palm at dummy arm from outdoor area
- **Lower Lying-Palm & Jut-Sau** (Movement 20) - Thrusting left palm at dummy trunk from under right arm while right arm poses Jut-sau movement

## Structure of the Wooden Dummy Form
The book indicates the 116 techniques are organized into distinct sections based on:
- **Position relative to dummy** (indoor area vs outdoor area)
- **Side of engagement** (left side vs right side)
- **Sequential progression** through the form

## Training Principles (from preface)
- The wooden dummy techniques form an essential part of Wing Tsun Kuen
- Techniques must be understood through proper application
- Traditional methods passed down from Grandmaster Yip Man
- Emphasis on correct positioning and movement execution
